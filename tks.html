Building Algorithms at TKS!!!

#4. TKS

It was now mid-October I finished my Open House video and needed a sustainable placement to finish the rest of my Co-Op. I had already expended all my Co-Op teacher's opportuntiies, it was now time for me to find something for myself.

I was part of an after school program called TKS where I had been working on many passions of mine such as explored Artificial Intelligence. I took a leap of faith and asked the program's director to take me in as a Co-Op student and for the first time in TKS history, there was a Co-Op student working there.

The Last Push

At TKS, I was an AI Algorithm Researcher/Developer. This was no easy task but it was immensely rewarding! My position was virtual as all of my tasks could be done virtually with the assistance of my director. However my director instilled the "figure it out" mindset into me early on so I only relied on myself when solving problems.

I also had to leverage media such as articles and videos to record my progress, on medium and youtube respectively. This is how I held myself accountable with my progress at TKS and it was helping me makes lots of progress. In the first few weeks, I had to learn the basics of calculus, and take a deep dive into using the Python programming language for Machine Learning. TKS was easily 1000 times harder than the programming I was doing at school in my Grade 11 Computer Science class, but it was also 1000 times more rewarding!

I worked on several things as part of TKS. My primary focus was Artificial Intelligence so I wrote multiple articles on my medium account, I made many algorithms. The one I was happiest with was my Heart Disease Prediction Heart Algorithm which could successfully predict whether a patient would have heart disease or not based off the patients data: systolic blood pressure, diastolic blood pressure, age, and a variety of other data. I also made a fun algorithm to see if the computer could predict which passengers died in the Titanic based off data collected upon the passengers.

Perhaps the most rewarding experience was working alongside Google to solve a marketting issue. As a TKS student, I was given this fine opportunity with my group to compete with other TKS teams in this challenge to see who could propose the best solution to Google. During this challenge, we proposed Adam the Ad Agent who would give personalized suggestions to each Google Ad's user along with a scoresheet implementation to easily point out benfits and flaws in advertising monthly for the user. This large challenge took two weeks and my team placed top 5 in all of Toronto with over 200 students participating.

TKS was highly valuable to me as a Co-Op experience. I think it was the perfect placement. There were no hazards as it was a virtual Co-Op and there were so many benefits. All of the problems faced were faced by me directly and not by the company which meant I could hold myself accountable the whole time. I gained many transferrable skills: how to leverage myself as an individual, critical thinking skills as I solved problems independently, time management skills due to the fact I was always under time pressure working on projects, and finally the confidence to work in the real world without feeling hesistant of my ability. TKS gave me every means to succeed and I operated on those terms. I never would have thought that as a 16 year old, I would be creating all these projects by myself. This is my greatest achievement of Co-Op I would say!
