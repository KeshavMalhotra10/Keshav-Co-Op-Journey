Message from the Principal?!

#3. A Project from The Principal

After leaving my Co-Op placement at Ideal Computers, I ended up searching for a new placement. Before I could get to that, I already had an offer from my Co-Op teacher who said I had a job directly from the principal!

I was to head to the library and await instructions from the school librarian. Upon reaching the library, I was told that my task was to create an open house video that celebrated the success of my school Senator O'Connor over the past year. This video was to be the main representation and video for the incoming students of Senator O'Connor to view and decide to join our wonderful school community. This was a huge opportunity! I could not refuse, and this opportunity ended up being my next Co-Op placement.

A Step in The Right Direction

After working an office job, this new opportunity was a breath of fresh air. I finally was able to fuel my creative vision by directing, filiming, and editing this video. The video was evidently shot at my school: Senator O'Connor College School in North York and this allowed me to be very active in discovering all the wonderful things happening at my school year round!

Compared to Ideal Computers, my previous Co-Op placement, this placement was relatively risk free. The only ways I could realistically get hurt were doing unsafe filming practices such as climbing trees to get good shots or doing running shots where I could fall.

This job was on my own terms, I got to learn so many different skills in such a short period of time. I only had 3 weeks before the Grade 8 Open House and had to film using my camera, edit with my macbook, and plan and direct the whole proccess. The tools I utilized were mainly GSUITE(Google Docs, Gmail) to plan everything out. Then the majority of my time was spent learning CapCut, an editing software I wanted to pick up during this project. Lastly, I got more experience directing others to work under me to help with the project. I got videographers and photographers to take the most exciting moments happening at our school within that time and send it all to me. They helped me improve my videography skills as well, and now I feel confident filiming videos!


Below I am using Capcut!!

The Open House Video was filled with decision making! Firstly, I had to think about the duration and length of the video which I couldn't have it too long to the point where I lost the audience, but I also needed to cover the whole year's worth of activities. I ended up deciding on a 3 minute video length that was packed with content after prioritizing the most relavent achievements of our school. Another major decision I made was the structure of the video, whether it be spread out with content like a highlights reel or more structured with each specific strength of our school touched upon such as sports, music, and academics. I ended up choosing the latter to make it as well thought out as possible.

After spending countless hours directing, compiling footage, and finally editing. I had a result I was proud of, now I just had to wait for the thoughts of those around me to see if my video had the impact I wanted to make.

The video ended up being an absolute success at the Open House with the audience being engaged. The video also did fairly well on youtube which was something else that made happy. I would say my favourite experience while filming this video was when Club Day at my school where I talked to all the different clubs and got them to cheer on their club. This moment had a lot of school spirit and I am glad I got to make it happen!
