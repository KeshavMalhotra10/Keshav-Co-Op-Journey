Why Co-Op, what’s in it for me?

#1. Why did I decide to do Co-Op?

Leaving Grade 10, I was extremely uncertain about my future. I was concerned about what my future held and where I would end up with my life. Just like everyone else, I always wanted to be successful but would I really be able to do that by the way things were going?

This question was one of my deepest worries, everyone around me were finding out their answers. Why was I slower than the rest, was I not good enough? These were common thoughts I experienced in my daily life and I wanted them to stop.

A Step in The Right Direction

After doing my research, I realized I could apply for my highschool's Co-Op program to be directly immersed in the fields I was thinking to be working in my future. This along with helping with my highschool SHSM requirements, Co-Op was a complete win-win scenario for me. I got to investigate into a question that had puzzled me for a long time, while also completing my obligations as a student.

After a long summer vacation, school had finally started again. With school, a wonderful opportunity to gain insight about my future! Let's get this straight, Co-Op is not like any other class. First think I noticed was how my Co-Op class took half of my schoolday(it should have been obvious because I would be in the workplace during this time), but I thought I found a way to exploit school.

I realized that if I found a place to work that made work seem like play, I would enjoy my Co-Op placement more than anything. The first challenge was to find a proper placement that I would enjoy. I was dreading the far too common retail job where I would be mindlessly doing tasks for months on end. I knew I wanted to use this time to better myself and wanted to be extremely selective of where I ended up.

Luckily enough, my Co-Op teacher was extremely thoughtful and passionate about getting me to where I wanted to be. I honestly wouldn't have gotten through Co-Op without her support, I remember her working tiredlessy to get me a tech placement that I really desired. I wanted to learn how to repair and fix computer hardware, she knew one place that offered this service called Ideal Computers and she made it her priority to get me there. With her help, I was able to get one of the first interviews in the class!

With this interview, I was able to convince the owner of the business that I was a Co-Op student who could add value to their business and I got my placement approved by him the day of meeting him. I remember being extremely happy as now I was directly working in the I.T field and seeing how operations work on to the day-day. In my next post, I will dive deep into my experience there :)
